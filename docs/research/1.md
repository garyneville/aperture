1. Seeing score replacement The best free, accessible API for astronomical seeing mentioned in the research is 7Timer!. It provides an "ASTRO" product that explicitly outputs astronomical seeing (in bins from <0.5" to >2.5") and transparency (in magnitudes per air mass) without requiring an API key. However, the exact endpoint URL pattern, accepted parameters, and update frequency for 7Timer! are not provided in the sources and would need to be verified independently.
If you must use your existing variables (CAPE, wind gusts, boundary-layer height, humidity) to create a 0–100 proxy score, you are working with a proxy for low-level turbulence rather than a true astronomical seeing calculation. Professional seeing models rely on upper-level winds (jet stream speed at 200–300 hPa) and steep thermal gradients (lapse rates > 0.5 K/100m) rather than surface humidity, which is "not very related" to seeing.
Because the sources do not provide an exact mathematical formula combining your specific variables into a 0-100 scale, you would need to build a heuristic penalty system based on the principles provided:

    Boundary-layer height: High boundary layers (especially coupled with high sensible heat flux) indicate deep turbulent mixing, which degrades seeing.
    Wind gusts & CAPE: High wind gusts (> 10 knots) create mechanical turbulence, while high CAPE indicates strong potential updrafts and thermal instability.
    A proxy formula would start at 100 (excellent) and apply aggressive subtraction penalties for high boundary layer heights, high wind gusts, and elevated CAPE.

3. Lightning risk derivation The sources do not provide a complete mathematical lookup table for a 0–100 lightning score, but they provide strict thresholds for UK and Northern Europe mid-latitude conditions:

    The CAPE Threshold: For lightning to occur, CAPE generally needs to exceed 500 J/kg, coupled with an Equilibrium Level (EL) temperature of < -10°C. Therefore, if CAPE is below 500 J/kg, your lightning risk is essentially zero.
    High-Shear/Low-CAPE Environment: In the UK and Northern Europe, high CAPE (>2000 J/kg) is rare. Organized convection and severe storms frequently occur in "lower-CAPE, high-shear environments".
    The Best Proxy: The most reliable index for European severe convection is WMAXSHEAR (the product of deep layer shear and the square root of 2 × CAPE). (Note: If you are using Open-Meteo for your data, you can bypass this manual derivation by requesting their native lightning_potential index (J/kg), which is available at 15-minute intervals).

4. Tide and swell for seascape scoring

    Tide API: The sources confirm that seascape scoring requires tide height and time, but they do not contain a specific free UK tide API, nor its request format or rate limits.
    Open-Meteo Marine: Open-Meteo's Marine API does cover the UK coast globally. It returns wave_height, wave_direction, wave_period, and wave_peak_period. For Europe, its highest spatial resolution is 0.05° (~5 km) using the DWD EWAM model, and 0.08° (~8 km) globally via MeteoFrance MFWAM.
    Ideal Swell/Tide states: The sources note that swell and tide are essential planning variables, but the specific "ideal" ranges for height, period, or mid-tide incoming states are not detailed in the sources and must be sourced externally.
